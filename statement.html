Statement of purposes

The purposes of the Blue Whale Study Inc. are:

(1) To conduct high-standard ecological research on blue whales, other marine species, and their feeding habitat in the Bonney Upwelling and adjacent waters, southern Australia.

(2) To work towards securing the recovery and long-term conservation of blue whales and the maintenance of their feeding habitat as a biologically diverse, productive ecosystem.

(3) To promote community, national and global awareness of blue whales, other marine species and their feeding habitat in this region, and to foster community participation in their long-term conservation and management.

(4) To work with other stakeholder groups in the region, including government and industry, to obtain the best possible outcomes for blue whales, other marine species, and their feeding habitat.

(5) To operate as a not-for-profit scientific research and educational organisation.

Mission statement

We are committed to the extension of knowledge in the field of natural science, in particular the conduct of original ecological research on the population of Endangered blue whales (Balaenoptera musculus), the largest of all living species, that migrates each summer to feed in continental shelf waters between Bass Strait and the Great Australian Bight, south-east Australia.

We aim to disseminate our findings through scientific publications and conferences, garner community and corporate support through community education, and highlight to the public the importance of protecting this extraordinary marine mammal and its habitat.
