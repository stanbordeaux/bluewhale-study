Methods

We conduct aerial surveys which show the distribution of the whales over time, and use satellite images of sea surface temperature and surface chlorophyll (ie phytoplankton) to enable us to monitor upwelling dynamics and biological productivity.

Our boat based studies are investigating the whales' prey (krill) and their behaviour, while temperature and conductivity data loggers throw light on the behaviour of the upwelling itself.

Pete's research has looked at the whole feeding area and Margie's current research examines blue whale habitat at a finer scale, using small vessels to conduct surveys in a focal area near the centre of the feeding ground.

We deploy passive acoustic loggers in order to study local blue whale calls in a global context, to indicate impacts from human activities, and allow tracking of blue whale movements.

To build on this multidisciplinary research, Pete and Margie collaborate with a number of research groups.
